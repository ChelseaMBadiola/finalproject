A Paradox

From the outside looking in, one might see my life as a paradox. From being a social science major at an engineering school to being a 5'2" athlete on a sports team that essentially requires height (volleyball), and everything in between, my life has been full of these seemingly conflicting characteristics. However, I've been able to extract meaning from a life of contradiction and balance...and I highly recommend it!

Education

Being a psychology major at a technology school has been one of the most rewarding experiences during my time as a student. After an attempt to minor in biology freshman year (and barely passing Genetics), I realized that I shouldn't ever force my brain to completely rewire itself just to do what everyone else is doing. I didn't need to minor in science to prove my worth.
Instead, I filled my free electives with classes I actually enjoyed. I took multiple philosophy courses where I learned all about artificial intelligence. Through my business courses, I learned about why people empathize with particular brands and how companies use that knowledge in order to advance their visions. While sitting in my architecture lectures, I realized the impact of physical space on our emotional experiences. In my Science and Technology class, my professor taught us how to think critically about existing technologies and how they are often formed based on social categorizations.
It wasn't until the end of my undergraduate career that I started to see how everything connects to the human experience. By taking classes that were challenging enough for me to feel enjoyment and growth at the same time, I was able to realize my potential. I know now that I want to be a an impactful social scientist in an evolving technological world.
